# What is the Science Generalization Hypothesis (SGH)?

*Published on March 2, 2025*

The **Science Generalization Hypothesis (SGH)** is a foundational ontological framework developed by independent researcher Yousef Yousefi. It seeks to answer the deepest questions about existence: why there is something rather than nothing, and why that something has the structure it does.

## Core Ideas

SGH proposes that all of reality emerges from a non-spatiotemporal **Metaphysical World (Ψ)** – a realm of pure potentiality. Through a lawful **extraction function**, this potentiality is actualized into dimensional matrices of space and time, which are then segmented and instantiated as physical entities.

Every entity, according to SGH, has two inseparable aspects:
- **Quantity (Substantial Object)** – its extension in space
- **Quality (Substantial Motion)** – its change in time

This duality is the foundation of the **Quantity–Quality Principle**, which grounds quantum uncertainty and the complementarity of wave and particle.

## Why It Matters

SGH resolves long-standing paradoxes in physics and mathematics, unifies abstract and concrete reality, and provides a coherent bridge between metaphysics and modern science. It is not just another physical theory – it is the ontological framework that makes any physical theory possible.
